Health Insurance Options We Offer

Individual & Family Plans (ACA Marketplace)

Comprehensive major medical coverage through the Health Insurance Marketplace. Includes essential health benefits and may qualify for subsidies.

  • Preventive care at no cost
  • Prescription drug coverage
  • Maternity and pediatric care
  • Mental health services
  • Subsidies available based on income

Best for: Self-employed, early retirees, those without employer coverage

Short-Term Health Insurance

Temporary coverage for gaps in health insurance (up to 12 months). Lower premiums than ACA plans but fewer benefits.

  • Fast approval (often same-day)
  • Lower monthly premiums
  • Coverage for unexpected illnesses/injuries
  • Not required to cover pre-existing conditions

Best for: Transition periods, waiting for employer coverage, COBRA alternative

Supplemental Health Insurance

Additional coverage to fill gaps in your primary health insurance—pays cash benefits directly to you for specific events.

  • Hospital Indemnity: Cash for hospital stays
  • Critical Illness: Lump sum for cancer, heart attack, stroke
  • Accident Insurance: Covers injuries from accidents
  • Cancer Insurance: Specific cancer treatment coverage

Best for: Supplementing high-deductible plans, extra protection

Medicare Planning

Expert guidance on Medicare enrollment, supplement plans, and Part D prescription drug coverage for those 65+ or disabled.

  • Medicare Advantage (Part C) plans
  • Medigap (Medicare Supplement) policies
  • Part D prescription drug plans
  • Enrollment timing and penalties

Best for: Those approaching 65, new Medicare enrollees

Understanding Health Insurance Terms

Premium

The amount you pay monthly for your insurance coverage, whether you use it or not.

Deductible

The amount you pay out-of-pocket before insurance starts covering costs. Example: $3,000 deductible means you pay the first $3,000.

Copay

A fixed amount you pay for specific services. Example: $30 for doctor visits, $15 for prescriptions.

Coinsurance

The percentage you pay after meeting your deductible. Example: 20% coinsurance means you pay 20%, insurance pays 80%.

Out-of-Pocket Maximum

The most you'll pay in a year. Once reached, insurance pays 100% of covered services.

Network

The doctors and hospitals contracted with your insurance. In-network = lower costs; out-of-network = higher costs or not covered.
